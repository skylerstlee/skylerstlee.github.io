I use computational tools to understand how proteins fold, interact, and fail — because those failures are often where disease begins.

My lab sits at the intersection of graph neural networks, molecular dynamics, and a stubborn belief that science should be reproducible and open.

Before New Geneva, I spent two years at DeepMind's biology division, and before that: too many nights in an MIT wet lab, staring at blots that never cooperated.

# research.log

Three active quests. Each one a different kind of puzzle.

⬡

ProteinGraph GNN

Building graph neural networks that predict protein–protein interactions at scale, moving beyond sequence similarity to structural and chemical reasoning.

GNN structural biology
progress
◈

MD Sampling

Developing accelerated sampling techniques for molecular dynamics that reduce compute time by 40×, making microsecond-scale simulations tractable on single GPUs.

ML molecular dynamics
progress
✦

OpenFold Audit

A systematic reproducibility audit of public protein structure predictions, building tooling and benchmarks the community can trust and extend.
